Rainbow City

The Rainbow City Project in Ugbisi town, is a beautiful plan to build one thousand (1,000) modern, decent, fine and affordable houses, for individuals, families and corporations in a peaceful environment. The hope is to promote good living in a clean, secure and organised estate.
Rainbow City was purposefully cited in Ugbisi town because it’s a clean, peaceful, crime free settlement with loving people, in Udu Kingdom, near Warri. It is also midway between Warri and Ughelli towns, which are major commercial centers in Delta State of Nigeria.
Besides, it is only five minutes away from the popular Ubogo market and ten minutes away from the new Ujewu Railway Station. Ugbisi indeed is a place where you can call, the new sweet home; and a great place of the future.

The Vision

The vision behind the Rainbow City, is the idea of building an elegant, first class housing estate, with an affordable budget. Everything is designed in a way to have the best houses, at the lowest prices, built with ease and at the lowest inconvenience; so we call this, a place of ‘value for money’.

Rainbow Housing and Social Policy

‘’Rainbow City concept’’ is the result of a corporate plan, to promote decent housing at an affordable rate; and to fight the common situation, where most people in Africa, live in filthy and terrible housing conditions; where families raise children in rugged, disorganised, criminalised, polluted and harsh inner-city environments.
In direct opposition to these weaknesses, the housing policy direction of Rainbow Network Afrique, is to continuously seek ways to promote vertical mobility, in housing matters, throughout Africa.
By this policy we do support and encourage (direct upward progress) for the population, in order to take more people out of the inner city, out of the Ghettos or Slums, and teach them how be their best and abandon low life, for a better life in the Rainbow Cities, for life.

‘Rainbow City’ is a Project and it’s also part of a Programme. The project part is the construction of buildings and development of real estates, in various ‘Rainbow Cities’, and these shall spread throughout Africa and beyond, in accord with our holding capacity.

Whereas, the programme part is, the promotion of a full scale war against poverty, homelessness, and living in filthy, polluted, degraded environments. This, we execute through public education, social mobilization and grass-root action.

Types of Houses

There are generally five types of houses that will be built, including

One bedroom, two bedroom, three bedroom and four bedroom or mixed use bungalows
One bedroom, two bedroom, three bedroom and four bedroom or mixed use one storey buildings
Two bedroom, three bedroom, four bedroom, five bedroom and six bedroom duplex buildings
Three bedroom, four bedroom and five bedroom duplex buildings / with two associated flats
Two bedroom and three bedroom terrace (duplex) houses
